**Semi-Structured Interview Guide: "High-Engagement, Low-Discovery" User Persona**

**Screener (5-7 minutes)**

To ensure we're speaking with the right persona, we'll start with a brief screener:

1. Can you tell us about your music listening habits? How often do you listen to music, and what types of devices or platforms do you use?
2. How many hours of music do you estimate you listen to per week?
3. Do you find yourself listening to the same songs or artists repeatedly, or do you try to mix it up?
4. Have you ever felt like you're stuck in a musical "rut" or that you're not discovering new music as often as you'd like?
5. On a scale of 1-5, how would you rate your satisfaction with Spotify's current recommendation algorithm (e.g., Discover Weekly, Release Radar)?

**Target criteria for the "High-Engagement, Low-Discovery" persona:**

* Listens to music for 10+ hours per week
* Self-identifies as someone who struggles to discover new music
* Rates their satisfaction with Spotify's current recommendation algorithm as 3 or lower

If the participant meets these criteria, we'll proceed with the core questions.

**Core Questions (30-40 minutes)**

1. **Current Frustrations:** Can you walk us through your current music discovery process on Spotify? What features do you use, and where do you usually go to find new music? What are some pain points or frustrations you've experienced with the current recommendation algorithm?
2. **Workarounds:** How do you currently try to discover new music outside of Spotify's recommendations? For example, do you follow music blogs, social media, or friends' playlists? What motivates you to explore these alternative sources?
3. **Ideal Discovery Experience:** If you could design your ideal music discovery experience on Spotify, what would it look like? What features or functionalities would you include, and how would they help you discover new music that resonates with you?
4. **Contextual Listening:** How does your music listening behavior change depending on your context (e.g., exercise, focus, relaxation, socializing)? Do you find that your current music discovery methods accommodate these different contexts, or are there gaps in your current experience?
5. **Novelty vs. Familiarity:** When it comes to music discovery, how important is it for you to find completely new and unexpected artists or songs versus discovering new music from artists or genres you already enjoy? Are there any specific moments or experiences where you prefer one over the other?
6. **Social Influence:** To what extent do you rely on social influences (e.g., friends, family, social media) to discover new music? Are there any specific social features or functionalities that you think would enhance your music discovery experience on Spotify?
7. **Goals and Motivations:** What drives your desire to discover new music? Is it to stay up-to-date with the latest trends, to explore different genres or moods, or something else? How does this motivation impact your current music listening habits and discovery processes?

**Additional Notes:**

* Throughout the conversation, take note of the participant's tone, language, and body language to gain a deeper understanding of their emotions and pain points.
* Feel free to ask follow-up questions or probe for more information to clarify any points or explore new avenues of discussion.
* Ensure that the participant feels comfortable sharing their honest opinions and experiences, and that the conversation remains focused on their needs and frustrations.
